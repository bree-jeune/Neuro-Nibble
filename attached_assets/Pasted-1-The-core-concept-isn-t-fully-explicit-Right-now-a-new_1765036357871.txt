1. The core concept isn’t fully explicit
Right now a new user has to infer:

“Tasks” → “Bites” → “Energy level” → “Week type (Gentle / Build / Repair / Chaos)”.
That the app is about touching tasks in micro‑steps, not “getting everything done”.
You hint at it (“Momentum in micro-doses”, “You can stop after this step. That still counts.”), but there’s no single, clear explanation.

What would help:

A 2–3 screen onboarding that spells out:
“Freeze → Break it down into bites → Match to your energy → Touch one bite → That counts.”
A short “What are bites?” explainer accessible from Tasks and the Break It Down screen.
A one‑line subheading on the Tasks tab like:
“Turn stuck tasks into 2–10 minute bites.”
2. Empty states and “what now?” moments
I don’t see any true empty states, but I can imagine these:

First time on Home or Tasks with no tasks yet.
First time on Reflect (not shown).
First time on You.
Without strong empty states, a new user can feel lost.

What would help:

Home empty state:
“Nothing here yet. What’s freezing you today?” → primary button “Break down a task”.
Tasks empty state:
“No bites yet. Add one thing that feels impossible right now.”
Reflect empty state:
“End a day to see how your bites add up over time.”
3. Guidance inside the flows
The Break It Down flow is conceptually great, but it still feels a bit “form‑y” and cognitive‑heavy:

“What’s freezing you?” + “What’s the tiniest first step?” + time + energy level.
No examples or suggestions when the user blanks.
What would help:

Inline examples under each field:
Under “What’s freezing you?” → “Ex: ‘grocery shopping’, ‘reply to landlord’, ‘start taxes’”.
Under “What’s the tiniest first step?” → “Ex: ‘open notes app’, ‘find last receipt’, ‘write subject line’”.
A “Suggest a tiny first step” chip that auto‑fills something plausible.
For time, pre‑set chips (2, 5, 10 min) instead of only +/-.
4. Consistency and hierarchy
Overall the visual design is cohesive, but a few things weaken clarity:

“Bites” vs “Steps” vs “Tasks”:
“0 of 2 bites complete”, “Steps done”, “task touched today” – three words for similar concepts.
Priority labels:
“Low / Medium / High” at top filters, and also badges on cards. That’s good, but the visual weight of “high” vs “medium” is subtle.
Week types:
“Chaos Week” is selected, but it’s not obvious how that choice affects what I see or do.
What would help:

Pick one vocabulary and stick to it:
e.g., “Task” + “Bites” (not “steps”).
Make the effect of Week type explicit:
After choosing Chaos Week, show a short banner:
“Chaos Week: Only the smallest bites count. Aim for 1–2 bites a day.”
Slightly stronger color or icon difference for high vs medium priority.
5. Control over tasks (basic CRUD feels incomplete)
From the screenshots, it’s unclear if users can:

Edit a task name, bites, time estimates, or energy level after creation.
Delete a task or a bite.
Reorder tasks or bites.
These are “must‑have” for a task app; without them, it feels like a prototype.

What would help:

Long‑press or swipe actions on task cards: Edit / Delete / Reorder.
Inside a task (grocery shopping screen), allow:
Reordering bites.
Editing bite text and time.
Deleting a bite.
6. Progress, history, and payoff
You have:

“End Day” flow.
“You” tab with total bites, steps done, completion %.
But there’s no sense of story over time:

What did I do yesterday vs today?
How did my energy levels correlate with progress?
What happened this week of “Chaos Week”?
Without that, the “End Day” ritual feels a bit detached from any long‑term reward.

What would help:

A simple history view:
List of past days with “bites touched” and energy level.
Weekly summary:
“This Chaos Week: 7 tasks touched, 12 bites done. That’s momentum.”
Tiny celebratory micro‑copy when ending the day:
You already have “You showed up today. That’s what matters.” – maybe add a small stat:
“You completed 1 bite today.”
7. Reflect / coaching layer (the “why this app” piece)
The app’s differentiator is clearly neurodivergent‑friendly, shame‑reducing micro‑steps. Right now, that philosophy is present but quiet.

What would help:

In Reflect (and maybe Home), short prompts like:
“What made today hard?” / “What helped you start?” with 1–2 tap answers.
Occasional micro‑coaching:
“You picked a 10‑minute bite on a low‑energy day. Want to shrink it to 2 minutes?”
This turns it from “just another task app” into a supportive tool.

8. Onboarding to energy + week types
Energy check‑in and week selection are powerful, but:

It’s not obvious how they change the experience.
A first‑time user might ignore them as “extra settings”.
What would help:

When user first chooses a week type, show:
“In Gentle Week, we’ll only ask you for 1 tiny bite a day.”
“In Build Week, we’ll suggest a few more bites if you have capacity.”
When they pick an energy level on Home:
Slight copy change on tasks:
Low energy → “Pick the easiest bite only.”
High energy → “You could do 2–3 bites if you want.”
9. Safety nets and feedback
Not visible from screenshots:

What happens if saving fails?
What if user leaves fields blank?
Is there an undo for “End Today” or “Mark done”?
These are small, but missing them makes an app feel fragile.

What would help:

Gentle validation:
If “What’s freezing you?” is empty, show:
“Try naming just one thing that feels heavy right now.”
Undo snackbars:
“Bite marked done. Undo.”
“Day ended. Undo.”
10. A clearer “first 5 minutes” path
Right now, a new user might:

Land on Home, see energy buttons, Chaos Week, and “clear phone storage”.
Tap Tasks and see a list.
Not be sure what to do next.
What would help:

First‑run checklist:
“Choose your week.”
“Add one frozen task.”
“Break it into 1–3 bites.”
“Do just the first bite.”
Once they’ve done that once, the app’s value becomes obvious.
